WhatsApp > WhatsApp Calling
A guide on how to use the WhatsApp calling feature directly on Pancake.
Pancake and Meta are testing the integration of WhatsApp Calls. This feature enables your WhatsApp Business page to receive or make calls with customers directly within Pancake.
1. What are WhatsApp Calls?
The WhatsApp Calling feature allows your business to receive incoming calls or make outgoing calls directly to customers on Pancake. It helps you:
	•	Support customers instantly in urgent situations (delivery, reservations, complaints).
	•	Build trust, as customers know they can reach you directly.
	•	Reduce reliance on external call centers by using WhatsApp as your main contact channel.

2. Who can use this feature? What's the limitation?
👉  Requirements:
	•	Business want to use initiated calling must NOT in the following countries: USA - Canada - Turkey - Egypt - Vietnam - Nigeria
	•	The phone number registered with WhatsApp Business API must have at least 1,000 business-initiated conversations within the last 24 hours (or higher)
👉  Limitations
	•	You can only initiated total 10 calls within 24hrs to your clients.
	•	With each clients, your business is limited to send 1 call request per 24 hours and 2 call requests per 7 days. This limit will reset once a successful call connection occurs.
3. Receive incoming calls on Pancake
Go to Call Settings in Pancake → Select WhatsApp.

4. Initiated a call to buyer
If you want to start a call to a WhatsApp user, your business must first receive the customer’s permission. 
✅ Within 24hrs windows
When the chat is inside the 24-hour window (counting from the customer’s last message) → you can send a freeform or template message to request a call.
✅ Outside 24hrs windows 
When the chat window is outside the 24-hour window (counting from the customer’s last message) → you must send a template message to request a call.
How to create a template message with a call request?
	•	Choose the appropriate template type:
	•	Utility (Recommended): Used for customer support purposes, such as confirming orders, table reservations, or resolving complaints.
	•	Marketing: Use only if the message includes promotional or advertising content.
2. Required component in the template message:
	•	Body: The main message content where you request the call.
Example: “We would like to call you to confirm your order. Do you agree?”
Note: 
- Keep the message short and neutral - avoid greetings or promotional tones. Do not use words like “allow”, “please tap”, or “permission.”
- Make it clear that this is a response or confirmation to an existing request (Meta only classifies it as a Utility message if it represents a “next step” in an ongoing process).
- Do not use emojis, exclamation marks, or special characters. You may use one line break, but avoid writing more than two lines in total.
	•	Header/Footer: Optional — you can include them to provide additional clarification if needed.
5. Pricing with business initiated calling

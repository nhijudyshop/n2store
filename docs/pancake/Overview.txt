Overview
The Overview section helps you quickly grasp the performance of your Page and team. From here, you can easily monitor sales performance, customer care efforts, and the effectiveness of ongoing campaigns — all visualized through easy-to-understand charts and metrics.
1. Date Range
Before viewing any data, you need to select a date range. This is a crucial step to ensure the data displayed is accurate. You can view statistics for Today or up to 180 days back.
https://statics.pancake.vn/pancake-docs/2025/4/27/iScreen Shoter - Google Chrome - 250427231715.jpg
🧠 Example: Charts in the Overview section will show data in 30 days, specifically from March 28, 2025 to April 27, 2025.
Note: 
Sometimes, the numbers in the detailed charts might be a bit different from the Overview chart. This is because each chart processes and groups the data in its own way. To get the most accurate insights, make sure you’ve selected the right time range.
2. Activity Overview
The statistical chart displays data based on the selected time range, including:
Total Interactions = Interactions with Returning Customers + Interactions with New Customers
Details: 
- An interaction is counted when your team is the first to message the customer on that day, either via inbox message or comment (or both).
- A customer is only counted once per day, even if multiple messages are sent.
	•	New Customers: The number of customers who messaged or commented on your Page for the first time.
	•	Orders: The number of confirmed orders recorded on Pancake.
https://statics.pancake.vn/pancake-docs/2025/4/27/iScreen Shoter - Google Chrome - 250427231805.jpg
💡 This activity overview chart helps you evaluate:
	•	Are your ads during this period generating effective results?
	•	Is customer interaction increasing?
	•	Are you attracting more new customers?
	•	Are most orders coming from new or returning customers?
3. Connection overview
This chart provides you with two key data points: Messages to Page and Comments on Page.
	•	Messages to Page: The total number of messages sent by customers to your Page. Each message sent = 1 count. A customer may send multiple messages.
	•	Comments on Page: The total number of comments sent by customers to your Page. Each comment sent = 1 count. A customer may leave multiple comments.
https://statics.pancake.vn/pancake-docs/2025/4/27/iScreen Shoter - Google Chrome - 250427231909.jpg
🧠 Example: The Page overview chart will display data in 30 days, specifically from March 28, 2025 to April 27, 2025, such as:
	•	Messages to connection: 172 (messages)
	•	Comments to connection: 66 (comments)
🔍 To view more detailed Page statistics, check out the Connection Statistics section .
4. Engagement
This chart displays statistics on how your team interacts with customers. It includes:
	•	Total Interactions: The total number of engaged with new and existing customers. 
	•	Messages: Total number of times the account engaged with customers via messages.
	•	Comments: Total number of times the account engaged with customers via comments.
	•	New inbox: Counted when a staff member messages a customer for the first time, it starts a Message Conversation. Even if the customer has only engaged through Comment Conversations before.

🧠 Example: The Engagement chart shows data in 30 days. For instance, from March 22, 2025 to April 21, 2025, the data would be:
	•	Total Engagement: 93
	•	Messages: 77
	•	Comments: 21
	•	New inbox: 0
🔍 You can explore the Engagement section in more detail to gain deeper insights .

	•	The "Average" is the average of all customer ratings during the selected time period. You can also see how this average compares to the previous time period.

The chart displays the total number of calls to the call center, including:
	•	Incoming Calls: Total number of calls received from customers.
	•	Outgoing Calls: Total number of calls made by employees to customers.
	•	The total number of calls is the average of all incoming and outgoing calls during the recent period.

9. User
This chart shows The top staff members who responded to customers the most, including:
	•	Message Response Sessions: The number of times staff and customers exchanged in a message conversation. One customer can have multiple sessions in a day.
	•	Comment Response Sessions: The number of times staff and customers' messages appear in a comment conversation. One customer can have multiple sessions in a day.

Engagement
The Engagement and Order Report on Pancake helps you track how well your staff are performing, how many customer engagements they’ve had, how many orders they’ve closed, and the conversion rates. This helps you easily evaluate staff performance and make informed decisions.
SUMMARY OF TERMS & FORMULAS
1. Selecting Data
Before checking any stats, you need to choose the staff and period you want to view. This helps filter out exactly what you need.
https://statics.pancake.vn/pancake-docs/2025/4/27/iScreen Shoter - Google Chrome - 250427171659.jpg
✅ Step 1: Choose "Customer engagement"
	•	Start by choosing the User (that means it's Staff )  whose engagement data you want to check.
	•	You can choose one, all, or multiple staff members (Hold Command + Click to select more than one).
✅ Step 2: Choose "Date Range"
Next, select the date or period you want to analyze.
You can view detailed data by hour, day, week, month, or year, depending on your needs.
🧠 Example: The statistics in the Engagement will be filtered for this month, specifically from April 1, 2025, to April 27, 2025.
 The time range in the "Overview" section might be different from the one in "Engagement". Make sure to double-check so you’re looking at the correct data.

3. Detail engagement statistics
This chart provides detailed information on engagement by time (day and hour) and by staff.
	•	By time: Engagement is displayed by day for the staff selected above.
	•	By staff: Engagement is shown for each staff member within the selected time range.
3.1 Customer engagement
The statistics in the "Customer engagement" table include:
	•	Engagement with old Customers: The number of times staff engage with old customers (customers who had been previously engaged) via both messages and comments.
	•	Engagement with New Customers: The number of times staff engage with new customers (customers who had not been previously engaged by any staff) via both messages and comments.
	•	Total Interaction with Old and New Customers: The total number of engagements staff had with customers (both new and old) via either comments or messages.
=>Total engagement = Engagement with Old Customers + Engagement with New Customers
	•	Messages: Total number of times staff engage with customers (new + old) via messages.
	•	Comments: Total number of times staff engage with customers (new + old) via comments.
	•	New Message Conversations: Customers who engage via a message conversation for the first time (even if they had previously engaged via comments).
All staff interactions must be performed via the Pancake system. If an engagement is sent outside of Pancake → it will not be counted in the statistics.
3.2 Confirmed Orders
When you filter order statistics from Pancake or POS, the detailed table below will automatically update accordingly.
The "Orders created" table includes the following metrics:
	•	New Customers: Number of orders created from new customers on that day.
	•	Old Customers: Number of orders created from returning customers on that day.
	•	Total Confirmed Orders (New + Old): The total of orders created from both new and old customers.
=> Total Orders created = Orders from New Customers + Orders from Old Customers
Note:
- If a new customer engages and creates an order on the same day → it counts as a New Customer Order.
- If a new customer engaged the previous day but creates an order the next day → it is counted as an Old customer Order.
	•	New customer order ratio: Created orders from new customers/ Total engagement with new customers.
	•	Old customer order ratio: Created orders from old customers/ Total engagement with old customers.
=> Average Conversion Rate = Total created orders/ Total customer engagement (both New + Old).
From these data points, you can easily assess your team’s performance in engaging with customers and the number of created orders they've completed.
